Decision Confidence in Human-Machine Teaming
Participant Information
Updated 4 February 2021

Welcome. We would like to invite you to take part in a research project. This document provides some information to help you decide whether you would like to do so. Please take the time to read this information carefully and consider whether you wish to take part. If you have questions about anything, please feel free to contact us .

In this study we are interested in understanding how the brain is organized, processes information and performs skills such as decision making. We can investigate this using computer-based behavioural tasks which are safe and non-invasive. Using many different techniques available on a computer program, we can gain information about human behaviour. We are interested in investigating the processes of decision making, and your confidence in those decisions.

During this study you will be performing computer-based tasks and will be asked to respond to stimuli appearing on the screen and to make those responses via mouse clicks.

You are free to withdraw from the study at any point. To do so, simply close your browser tab. There will be no penalty for doing so.

We collect data on your performance during the study. Any information collected about you during this study will be kept strictly confidential. Any data (including task performance/questionnaire answers) will be anonymised with a code so that the results can not be linked back to you. All the data is kept on firewall and password-protected computers. Any personal information about you will be destroyed at the end of the study. Sometimes, new methods to analyze previously collected data become available after a study has ended. Therefore, we ask for your permission to use your anonymised data in future studies, and to share data with other researchers both inside and outside of the European Union. Responsible members of the University of Oxford may be given access to your data for monitoring and/or the audit of the study to ensure we are complying with relevant guidelines.

Personal data, specifically IP addresses, are logged by the server as part of the normal process of providing a secure and stable service. The University of Oxford is the data controller with respect to these data. Your personal data are stored separately, and will not be linked to your performance. Your personal data will not be shared, and only responsible members of the University of Oxford will be allowed access for monitoring, audit, or server administration purposes.

Payment rates and conditions, where applicable, will have been made clear previously. If you are unsure about payment, please contact us .

All research studies are reviewed by an ethics committee to ensure the research is conducted safely and to the best standards. This research has been reviewed by, and granted ethics approval through the Ministry of Defence Research Ethics Committee (MoDREC) and Oxford University.

If you have a concern about any aspect of this project, please speak to the researcher, Matt Davidson [matthew.davidson@psy.ox.ac.uk], or the principal investigator, Prof Nick Yeung [nicholas.yeung@psy.ox.ac.uk], who will do their best to answer your query. The researcher should acknowledge your concern within 10 working days and give you an indication of how they intend to deal with it. If you remain unhappy or wish to make a formal complaint, please contact the Chair of the Research Ethics Committee at the University of Oxford: Chair, Medical Sciences Inter-Divisional Research Ethics Committee; Email: ethics@medsci.ox.ac.uk; Address: Research Services, University of Oxford, Wellington Square, Oxford OX1 2JD.

This research is organised by the Attention and Cognitive Control Laboratory at Oxford University, under the direction of Prof Nick Yeung, who is a Professor of Cognitive Neuroscience. The study is funded by a grant from the Defence Science and Technology Laboratory (an executive agency, sponsored by the UK Ministry of Defence) on a project entitled "Adaptive Joint Cognitive Systems for Complex and Strategic Decision Making".

This study complies and at all times will comply, with the Declaration of Helsinki as adopted at the 64th WMA General Assembly at Fortaleza, Brazil in October 2013.
